# Council Review Rubric

Use this rubric before the build and during reviews. It is the baseline for comparing feedback across council members and across artifacts.

## Scoring scale

Use a 1 to 5 scale.

- **1 = weak / unclear / poor**
- **2 = below standard**
- **3 = acceptable but needs work**
- **4 = strong**
- **5 = excellent**

## Universal criteria

These apply to almost every artifact type.

### 1. Clarity
Does the artifact say what it is trying to say clearly?

Questions:
- Is the core idea easy to understand?
- Is the language direct?
- Is anything confusing or muddy?

### 2. Specificity
Is it concrete enough to act on?

Questions:
- Is it too abstract?
- Are claims supported by specifics?
- Are decisions clearly named?

### 3. Differentiation
Does it stand apart from generic alternatives?

Questions:
- Why this and not something generic?
- Is there a clear angle or edge?
- Does it avoid commodity framing?

### 4. Audience Fit
Is it built for the right audience?

Questions:
- Who is this for?
- Would that audience understand and care?
- Is the tone aligned with the audience?

### 5. Practical Value
Is it useful in the real world?

Questions:
- Does it help someone do, decide, or understand something?
- Is it actionable?
- Is it too theoretical?

### 6. Trust / Credibility
Does it feel believable, grounded, and worth paying attention to?

Questions:
- Does it overclaim?
- Does it sound generic or hypey?
- Would a smart reader/viewer trust it?

### 7. Focus
Is it disciplined, or is it trying to do too much?

Questions:
- What should be cut?
- What is unnecessary?
- Does the artifact stay on one lane?

### 8. Memorability
Will anything about it stick?

Questions:
- Is there a strong point of view?
- Is there a strong hook?
- Is the artifact forgettable?

## Artifact-specific criteria

## Ideas / Concepts

### 9. Problem Strength
- Is there a real problem here?
- Is it painful or valuable enough?

### 10. Solution Coherence
- Does the solution make sense?
- Is the product/service shape coherent?

### 11. Strategic Potential
- Could this become something durable?
- Is it a hobby idea or a real opportunity?

## PRDs / Strategy Docs

### 9. Scope Discipline
- Is V1 realistic?
- Is the scope too broad?

### 10. Sequencing
- Is the order of operations sensible?
- Is anything being built too early?

### 11. Feasibility
- Is the plan technically and operationally realistic?

## Blog Posts / Articles

### 9. Point of View
- Does it say anything interesting?
- Is it just obvious?

### 10. Readability
- Would someone actually finish it?
- Is it tight enough?

### 11. Insight Density
- Is there enough value per paragraph?

## Scripts / Shorts

### 9. Hook Strength
- Does the opening create attention?

### 10. Flow / Pacing
- Does it move cleanly?

### 11. Shareability
- Would someone save or share this?

## Landing Pages / Positioning

### 9. Category Clarity
- Is it obvious what category this belongs in?

### 10. Message-Market Fit
- Is the promise clear and relevant?

### 11. Conversion Potential
- Would this persuade the right person to take the next step?

## Output expectations

Every review should produce:

- 3 strongest points
- 3 weakest points
- 1 thing to cut
- 1 thing to sharpen
- 1 biggest unanswered question
- overall score
- go / revise / pause / reject recommendation

## Red flags rubric

Regardless of score, flag these immediately if present:

- generic positioning
- unclear audience
- too much scope
- fake differentiation
- excessive hype
- weak trust signals
- no actual point of view
- unclear next step
